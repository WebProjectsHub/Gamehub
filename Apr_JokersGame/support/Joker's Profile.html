Character – The Joker Killer

Real Identity (Unknown to Detectives)

Formerly a regular office worker with no criminal history.

Background & Origin

  • Lived a mundane life working a desk job, enjoying puzzles as a hobby.
  • Created intricate riddles and logic games, but lacked an audience.
  • During a police operation involving the captain’s friend, a shootout occurred.
  • His wife was accidentally killed as a bystander in the incident.
  • The arresting officer: the captain’s now-deceased friend.
  • The tragedy drove him to madness, weaponizing his passion for puzzles.

Motivation

  • Seeks revenge on the police system he blames for his wife’s death.
  • Wants to make detectives play his game to expose their flaws.
  • Believes puzzles reveal truth; failure to solve them proves unworthiness.
  • Ultimate target: the captain. Everyone else is part of the warm-up.

Compulsion / Killer Profile

  • Highly intelligent and methodical.
  • Compelled to build elaborate, logic-based puzzles.
  • Leaves a calling card at each scene—usually a playing card or cryptic puzzle.
  • Views victims as pieces in a larger game.
  • Only targeted victim so far: Daniel Keller, a journalist linked to the shootout’s legacy.

Signature Elements

  • Employs UV ink, ciphers, anagrams, and multi-layer riddles.
  • Messages contain double meanings and symbolic clues.
  • Uses time constraints (e.g., “24 hours to solve”) to escalate tension.
  • Enjoys toying with law enforcement more than simply killing.
