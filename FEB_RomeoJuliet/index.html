Romeo and Juliet
"Romeo and Juliet" is a tragic love story by Shakespeare, where two young people from feuding families, the Montagues and Capulets, fall passionately in love. Their forbidden romance leads to secret marriage, a series of unfortunate events, and ultimately, their untimely deaths, which finally end the families' long-standing conflict.
Trivia
The idea of "star crossed lovers" is a very old one, and can be seen in stories from ancient times, such as the story of Pyramus and Thisbe. So Romeo and Juliet is part of a very long line of tragic love stories.
Trivia
While the exact composition of Juliet's potion is fictional, the concept itself is rooted in historical knowledge and beliefs about the power of certain substances to alter the body's state, even to the point of mimicking death.
Trivia
The families of Montague and Capulet, or Montecchi and Cappelletti, are believed to have some historical basis, with mentions found in Dante's "Divine Comedy."
